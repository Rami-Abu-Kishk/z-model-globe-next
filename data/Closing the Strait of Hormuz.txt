First: Impact of Closing the Strait of Hormuz on the UAE (2026)
1) Security and Geopolitical Impact
The closure of the Strait of Hormuz is considered one of the highest-risk geopolitical escalation scenarios in the Gulf because:
* It directly threatens UAE maritime security (Dubai – Abu Dhabi – Jebel Ali) 
* It disrupts energy and maritime trade flows 
* It increases the likelihood of regional military confrontation 
 Quantitative assessment:
Security impact on the UAE: 95% (Critical / Near Maximum)
Reason: extremely high dependence on a vital maritime corridor

* Major disruption to Gulf oil and gas exports 
* Sharp rise in shipping and insurance costs 
* Disruption of global supply chains via the UAE as a re-export hub 
Quantitative assessment:
Economic impact: 92% (Critical)
Reason: the UAE is a global logistics hub heavily dependent on maritime trade


3) Strategic Impact
* Reshaping global energy strategies 
* Acceleration of alternative export routes (pipelines – ports outside the Gulf) 
* Strong pressure on maritime defense infrastructure
